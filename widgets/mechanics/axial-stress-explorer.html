Axial Stress

MECHANICS OF MATERIALS · Normal Stress Under Uniaxial Tension

When a force \(F\) is applied along the centroidal axis of a prismatic member, a uniform normal stress develops across every cross section:

$$\sigma = \frac{F}{A}$$

Because stress is force per unit area, the convenient engineering unit is MPa = N/mm². If \(F\) is in kN and \(A\) in mm², multiply by 1000 first: \(\sigma\,[\text{MPa}] = F\,[\text{kN}] \times 1000 \;/\; A\,[\text{mm}^2]\).

Tension Diagram

Equal and opposite forces stretch the member along its axis. A section cut reveals the stress — every mm² of cross-sectional area carries exactly the same load. The bar color deepens as stress increases.

Positive \(\sigma\) means tension — the member is being pulled. The internal stress is the same at every cross section along the member length; this is what makes axially loaded members analytically clean.
