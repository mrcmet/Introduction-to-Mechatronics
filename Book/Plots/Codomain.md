#GraphTheory #Definition 
# **Definition**

---

> [!tldr]
> In engineering plots, a **codomain** is the physical quantity represented on the dependent axis of a graph. The codomain defines _what is being measured, observed, or produced by a system_ in response to changes in the domain. Common codomains include voltage, displacement, force, temperature, strain, pressure, and amplitude.

A codomain represents the system’s response and gives meaning to the numerical values produced by measurement or modeling.


# **Key Ideas**

---

- The codomain is typically plotted on the vertical (y) axis
    <br>
- A valid codomain corresponds to a real physical quantity
    <br>
- Codomains always carry units and scale
    <br>
- The codomain depends on the system being observed, not on how data is indexed
    <br>
- Interpretation of system behavior depends on understanding the codomain
    

## **Codomain vs Domain**

---

- **[[Domains]]**: what is independently varied or controlled
    <br>
- **Codomain**: what is measured or observed as a result
    <br>

Together, the domain and codomain define the relationship being visualized and determine whether a plot is exploratory or predictive.

  

##  **Codomain vs Signal Representation**

---

The same physical phenomenon may appear as different codomains depending on how it is measured or processed.

  
For example:

- A mechanical vibration may appear as displacement, velocity, or acceleration
    <br>
- An electrical signal may appear as voltage, current, or power
    

The codomain must always be interpreted in the context of the measurement method and system model.

Codomains are commonly used in an [[Effects plot]] and in a [[Domain Plot]].
